UNIT 11: FUTURE TRENDS IN SECURE SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT

Reflection

We had the chance to have a look at upcoming developments in software development in this unit, with a focus on the shift from monolithic structures to microservices. The reading material gave readers a historical perspective on how structures have changed over time. I found it very fascinating to learn about ideas like mobile edge computing and fog computing. Since there was not much reading required for this unit, the assignment submission was the most exciting aspect of this week. As a team, we were able to finish our project and submit it on time.

Team Activity

Debate: Microservices and Microkernels

Read Appendix A: the Tanenbaum-Torvalds debate in DiBona & Ockman (1999) then read Fritzsch et al (2019). The forum has a message that says: “Torvalds has been proven wrong and it only took nearly thirty years. Microservices and microkernels are the future. “

Skills/Knowledge Gained

  • In a decentralized computing environment known as fog computing, data, compute, storage, and applications are distributed between the data source and the cloud.
  • A sort of network architecture called multi-access edge computing (MEC) offers cloud computing capabilities and an IT service environment at the network's edge.
  • While cloud computing enables us to store and manage our data and information, mobile computing allows us to access data and information via a mobile device.
  • A collection of virtualization platforms with a variety of capabilities makes up the superfluid cloud.
  • Latency of security mechanisms is the biggest challenge to secure the edge paradigms.
