UNIT 3: UML

This lesson dives right into the Unified Modelling Language (UML), a tool for designing software systems before they are put into code. It is divided into three categories: architectural models, structural diagrams, and behavioral diagrams. In conclusion, certain UML diagrams reflect the behavior of the system, while other diagrams analyze and depict the software's logical structure.

COLLABORATIVE DISCUSSION

E-PORTFOLIO ACTIVITIES

Question

Discuss which UML models are most applicable at different stages of the Software Development Life Cycle.

Solution:

The use case diagram and activity diagram are the most often used behavioral diagrams, and they are in charge of the interaction across time between software components. Contrarily, structural diagrams show the interrelationships between the system's constituent parts. The class diagram is the structural diagram that is used the most. Architectural Models, which also show the software system's structure, encompass structural and behavioral features.
