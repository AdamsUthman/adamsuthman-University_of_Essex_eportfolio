UNIT 12: WORKING WITH DESIGN PATTERNS TO STRUCTURE CODE

The submission of the e-Portfolio will serve as the final assignment for the module's final unit. The unit will also look at how design patterns may be used to organize code. I had the chance to delve further into some of the design patterns in the module's final unit, particularly the Singleton, Decorator, State, Strategy, and Observer patterns. The argument made by Phillips (2018) caught my attention in particular: while the 23 patterns suggested by the Gand of Four are undoubtedly applicable to Python, it frequently has a different (and typically simpler) way to do the same task, as is shown from the Decorator pattern.
